About

Jim is a recent transplant to the greater Boston area. Originally from northeastern Ohio, he graduated with this bachelor of science in Computer Information Systems from Youngstown State University in December of 2019.

While at YSU, Jim was an active member of multiple organizations of which he is quite proud. He participated in multiple hackathons and was secretary of the student chapter of Association for Computing Machinery. He also participated in school and community outreach programs such as the Oak Hill Collaborative and Code Youngstown, which strove to build and network with local businesses and entrepreneurs looking to expand into the area via the local business incubator.

Jim's internship, while in school, was with the YSU Department of Web and Creative Services as a web developer. In addition to being able to work very closely with the full-time staff, of which one was an adjunct professor who had also been h python instructor, the open floorplan of the office put the team in an ideal situation for Q/A as well as impromptu lessons on frameworks, bootstrap, flexbox, and the like for more on-the-job instruction.

Supplementary to Jim's internship, he spent 3 years working with the Oak Hill Collaborative as part of the "Success After Six," program, where he was a Python Instructor. Through this program, they taught inner-city school children, between 4th and 6th grade, the principles of programming.

All, while maintaining a 3.52 GPA.
